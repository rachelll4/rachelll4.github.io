About Me

I graduated from the University of Pittsburgh at Johnstown in 2019 with a BS in Computer Engineering. While I was completing my undergrad, I worked consistently for my student newspaper, The Advocate, and served in News, Features and Opinions page editor positions. I also was a constant staff writer and copy editor. I loved the paper, even between group coding projects in C# and Java, and tried to keep both in my life.

After graduation, I worked as a software consultant for a small company, picking up Python and OpenCV. I helped to tinker and tweak a pill-counting machine, working on not only the motors, cameras and sensors, but also a new user interface and video-processing logic.

But I didn't have time to write, as well. I decided I needed to feed both halves of my brain.

So I made the leap. I completed a masters degree in journalism at the University of Maryland in May 2022. I focused on data journalism, but also learned web development for the first time. I worked on some incredible, collaborative investigations for the Howard Center, some of which even won awards. I made news apps for exploring databases of findings. I crafted interactive maps aplenty. I taught myself JavaScript over winter break, and a world opened up to me.

Out of grad school, I was picked up by USA TODAY Network's Storytelling Studio, a team of brilliant devlopers making new, wild, bold interactives and web components and databases to support digital article presentations. My understanding of JS and CSS exploded. I learned Svelte, Node, Golang and Scrollama. I made scolling narratives that twist maps and models at each step to visually explain investigations. I made it easier for series of articles to pull the same data powering recirculation. I even made some data graphics.

As always, I'm looking forward to what puzzles the future brings. No matter where I end up or what community I end up serving, I know I'll be fulfilling a calling, creating change and empowering my fellow humans. We're called to take no part in the unfruitful works of darkness, but instead expose them, and that's what I plan to do!
